Summary: Trading Behavior vs Market Sentiment

Methodology:
The analysis combines historical trading data with the Fear & Greed Index to study how market sentiment impacts trader performance and behavior.

First, both datasets were cleaned and preprocessed. Timestamps were converted into a common date format so that the data could be aligned at a daily level. The datasets were then merged using the date column.

Next, key metrics were created, including average PnL (profitability), win rate (percentage of profitable trades), trade frequency (number of trades), average trade size, and leverage as a risk indicator.

Traders were also divided into two groups: frequent traders and infrequent traders, based on their trading activity.

Key Insights:
Trader performance varies depending on market sentiment. Profitability and win rates are different during Fear and Greed conditions.

Trader behavior also changes with sentiment. The number of trades and the size of positions increase or decrease based on market conditions, showing that traders adjust their strategies.

Risk patterns differ across traders. Frequent traders tend to take different levels of risk compared to infrequent traders, especially during volatile market situations.

Strategy Recommendations:
During Fear markets, traders should reduce leverage to minimize risk and avoid excessive trading due to uncertainty.

During Greed markets, traders should control position sizes to avoid overexposure and maintain proper risk management even when the market looks favorable.

Overall, aligning trading strategies with market sentiment can help improve decision-making and reduce risk.
